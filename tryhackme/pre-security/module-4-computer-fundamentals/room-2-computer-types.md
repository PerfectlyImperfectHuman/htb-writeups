## Key concepts learned

Different Types of Computers

- Laptop — A portable computer that is used for everyday tasks like emailing or messaging someone or watching a YouTube Video. Since they are smaller and portable they have a battery that can run out and they have a small and compacted cooling system due to which the laptops overheats a lot when doing a heavy task
- Desktop — Are fixed in one place, used for everyday tasks and heavy tasks ( how heavy depends on the specs of the computer being used ), since they get wall power supply they can run as long as they are receiving the power and they have much more space and hence a much larger and better cooling system so they overheat much less
- Work-Station — A type of a desktop that is mainly used for work or heavy stuff, it is built in a different way from a regular desktop and it focuses on accuracy, performance and reliability and is used for complex and long calcuations
- Server — It is a type of computer that doesn't have any input or output devices it is used to provide services across a network and these are very powerful and run 24/7 answering all of the requests from users and providing the services needed
- Smartphones — They are pocket-sized computers built for everyday tasks, communication and optimized for battery life. Due to their small size you can carry them around anywhere and they are the most common type of computers being used today
- Tablet — Same as a smartphone but with a bigger screen, they are mostly used for Art and Drawing
- Interent of Things (IoT) Device — It is used to do some small specialized tasks and it is connected to the Internet and can communicate with other devices connected to the network. Example: A Smart-Watch can do some tasks like calculation, time, step tracking, sending messages and receiving calls and they can also connect to the internet and communicate with mobiles and other devices
- Embedded Computers — Just like IoT devices they are also used to perform mostly a single specialized task but the difference is that they are not connected to a network or the Internet they simply perform the task. For Example: My water bottle has an embedded computer in it that tells the temprature when you touch the cap and nothing else, similarly a calculator only does calculations etc
